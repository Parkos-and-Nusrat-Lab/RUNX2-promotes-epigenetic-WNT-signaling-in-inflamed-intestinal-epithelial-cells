# RUNX2-promotes-epigenetic-WNT-signaling-in-UC-epithelium
Repository for the code to replicate the figure on the paper "RUNX2 promotes epigenetic WNT signaling in UC epithelium"

## Abstract
Ulcerative colitis (UC), a subtype of inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), is characterized by chronic mucosal inflammation, recurrent epithelial injury, and impaired wound healing in the colon. While dysregulation of WNT/β-catenin signaling has been reported in IBD, the mechanisms linking these signaling abnormalities to IBD pathogenesis remain unclear. To investigate epithelial intrinsic changes in UC, we performed single nucleus RNA-seq (snRNA-seq) and ATAC-seq (snATAC-seq) multiomics on human primary colonic epithelial cells (colonoids) derived from healthy individuals, as well as donors with inactive or active UC, cultured in 3D matrix recapitulating crypt base cells (undifferentiated) and as 2D monolayers in differentiation medium (differentiated luminal epithelial cells). Colonoids from active UC showed a distinct population with transcriptional profile in 3D culture, marked by elevated CTNNB1 expression and reduced APC. Chromatin accessibility analysis identified enrichment of RUNX2 motifs specifically in this UC-associated cell population. Colonoids from active UC also exhibited reduced expression of the stem cell marker OLFM4 in 3D and the differentiation marker VIL1 in 2D, suggesting impaired self-renewal and maturation. RUNX2 inhibition using CADD522 reduced β-catenin levels in 3D colonoids and restored VIL1 expression and junctional β-catenin localization in 2D cultures. These findings reveal an intrinsic defect in epithelial renewal in UC, driven in part by RUNX2-dependent WNT dysregulation. Our study identifies RUNX2 as a transcriptional regulator of epithelial stem cell function and WNT signaling in the inflamed human colon.
